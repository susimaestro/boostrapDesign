discovery

1.1 USER RESEARCH

To uncover pain-points and needs of the user I conducted 2 surveys and 4 interviews with the Tiffin owners, their clients and general café goers.

1.2 DESK RESEARCH

Through the interviews and surveys I felt I had a much better idea of Tiffin. I got to know about their background, motivations, values and mottos. Now it was time to see how that translated to their online presence. The only online platform they used was Instagram.

1.3 BUSINESS & COMPETITION RESEARCH

From the interview process, we uncovered some of the competitors and compared them to the café with different tools like Business Model Canvas, Brand Opposites analysis, Market Positioning Map and so on. This gave a great understanding of their position in their market and gave some ideas on how they could improve their visibility. Their competitors seem to understand the importance of branding and having a strong online presence.

defining

2.1 Affinity diagram

An affinity diagram really helped with creating, desiging, and consolidating the data from the research process. It's very useful to get a clear overview of the information.

2.2 User personas

User personas create understanding and empathy with the users. It helps to step out of yourself and recognize that different people have different needs and expectations.

2.3 User journeys

User journey can be used to clearly explain the strengths and weaknesses of the product in terms of UX. It shows the emotional reactions the user has as they use your product

2.4 User stories

User stories are representations of small instances in peoples’ lives. The purpose is to generate ideas that fit into the user’s life. They are told from the perspective of the user and written in plain English.

2.5 Testing the website

How would 4 users who had never seen the NOS Play website interact with it the first time? Based on what the NOS clients said they struggled with, I made up a list of tasks for the new users to do, asked them to think out loud as they were going through it, screen recorded the sessions and finally asked them how they think it went in the end.

2.6 HMW

With all the research in mind we can now narrow it down to one simple HMW queston, which will be the foundation of the upcoming phase 'Ideate'

"How might we make NOS Play an engaging, user-friendly and intuitive experience?"

4.1 Low-fidelity prototype - Mobile Onboarding

It's time to put our ideas to the test!"Fail fast" is something Product Designers often say. Drawing a rapid prototype to get early feedback from testing with users can save you a lot of time and money down the line and prevent you from developing bad ideas further

On the right is the first low-fidelity prototype of the mobile Onboarding experience.

Changes after first test:
- Adding the option to go back in case of error.
- The option to change genre was also put before picking movies so the movie option would correspond to your genre preference.

4.2 Mid-fidelity prototype - Mobile Onboarding
To the left is the first Mid-fi prototype. I made a prototype on the platform Useberry, which allows you test your design with your users and shows you a video of where they clicked. Very useful to see what's intuitive to the users!

Changes that were made:
- Elements were made smaller
- Rectangles changed to NOS style
- Adding go back options
- Adding 'skip' option

testing

Testing is a part of the Prototype phase as you often end up iterating and going back and forth. The golden rule is: the more you test the more fool proof your results will be, so it was important in this stage to test with as many people and age groups as possible to see how intuitive the design was. Around 15 to 20 different people tested each phase of the design.

Useberry makes it easy with screen recording your users actions going through your designs and showing you where they were more prone to click with the heatmaps.
