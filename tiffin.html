Tiffin Café

Tiffin is a family run café in the center of Lisbon serving plant based food made with love. They wanted to increase their online visibility with an E-commerce site, but on their own terms making sure the style felt like them.

TIMELINE

April- May 2020 using the Agile method and delivering protoypes

ROLE


We were three UX designer doing the user research, then my role was to take care of the competitive and business research as well as wireframes, ideating, branding, design, prototypes and preparing assets for the developer .

PROBLEM

How can we help them increase their online precense in a way that they feel correctly represented?

GOAL

Designing a web page that had their core values of honesty, authenticity and community at heart.

SOLUTION

a simple and straightforward web page reflecting all the values that were important to the café

1. discovery

1.1 USER RESEARCH

To uncover pain-points and needs of the user we conducted 2 surveys and 4 interviews with the Tiffin owners, their clients and general café goers.

1.2 DESK RESEARCH

Through the interviews and surveys we felt we had a much better idea of Tiffin. We got to know about their background, motivations, values and mottos. Now it was time to see how that translated to their online presence. The only online platform they used was Instagram.

1.3 BUSINESS & COMPETITION RESEARCH

From the interview process, we uncovered some of the competitors and compared them to the café with different tools like Business Model Canvas, Brand Opposites analysis, Market Positioning Map and so on. This gave a great understanding of their position in their market and gave some ideas on how they could improve their visibility. Their competitors seem to understand the importance of branding and having a strong online presence.

2. defining

2.1 Affinity diagram

An affinity diagram really helped with creating, desiging, and consolidating the data from the research process. It's very useful to get a clear overview of the information.

2.2 User flows

User flows are diagrams that display the complete path a user takes when using a product. The user flow lays out the user's movement through the product, mapping out each and every step the user takes—from entry point right through to the final interaction

2.3 Concept sketching

Sketching can be a very efficient way of communicating design while allowing me to try out a multitude of ideas and iterate them before settling on one.

2.4 Sitemap

A site map is a visual hierarchy of the website, that shows how pages are prioritized, linked, and labeled. It's great to give you an overview of the options you have on the website

2.5 Card sorting

Card sorting is a technique in user experience design in which a person tests a group of subject experts or users to generate a dendrogram or folksonomy. This really helped in organizing the website and the navigation!

2.6 HMW

With all the research in mind I now narrow it down to one simple HMW queston, which will be the foundation of the upcoming phase 'Ideate'

How might we help them increase their online precense in a way that they feel correctly represented?

3. ideate

3.1 Brainstorming design opportunities

Brainstorming is always my favourite part of the process. You put the HMW question on the top and come up ideas that might be the solution. Put on the Rocky music and anything goes. This process is quite messy so afterwards you look through everything, and stick with the idea that were the best ones.

4. prototyping & testing

In this phase I drew and designed Low-fi and Mid-fi prototypes and tested them with users on the Useberry platform. The user were given a specific happy path to follow and their experience was recorded. I noted down what felt intuitive for them and also asked them for additional feedback to improve the app, and made changes accordingly.

Desktop Low-fi

Tiffin were pretty flexible in terms of what style they wanted on the website, so we started with looking at what the competition was doing, did our own version and asked users what they liked and what they didn't like.

Desktop Mid-fi

A lot of changes were made to make this mid-fi more functional in terms of Nielsen's Usability Heauristic Principles tackling error prevention, efficiency of use etc. I did my best to make the mid-fi as intuitive as possible.

Mobile Mid-fi

The changes made to the mobile version were mainly due to the sizing of the elements. Other than that the users found the app very straightforward to use.
